angel-grating-report

Testing new grating setups

G0 and G1 are fixed, and are the same as in the previous experiments that we ran in the Summer. This achieved about 15% median visibility.

G0 is 1172-3.

G1 is 1093-6.

The new absorption gratings are tested for their performance as G2's in the following. The detector is the Eiger 1M with a silicon sensor, tube settings: 100 kVp, 6 mA, 1 mm focal spot. 10 phase steps, 1 s exposure per step.

The region of interest shown in the pictures is a rectangle with [350:750, 600:1000] pixels from the detector area.

A visibility map is show for each of the new gratings, with an absorption map besides. Strong deformations in the golden structures are seen in 1706 and 1707, which make those probably unfit for imaging altogether. Minor defects are visible in 1638 and similar punctures appear in a somewhat more sever amounts in 1629. 1651 on the other hand achieved very good uniformity.

The single grating scans are absorption pictures taken with only one grating on the beam and 10 s exposure. The roi is slightly larger [150:1000, 400:1000]. The horizontal white band is a gap between detector modules.
